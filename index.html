About iSWIM

iSWIM is a subsidiary of TRM (HK) Limited, a company with more than 20 years of experience in handling employees’ compensation claims in Hong Kong.

We understand the difficulties and headache in handling insurance claims. That's why we developed a smart work injury management system (SWIM) — a platform to monitor sick leave and predict permanent disability. The system also uses artificial intelligence to help develop strategies to handle insurance claims.

This project was done in collaboration with Hong Kong Polytechnic University and has received an Innovative Technology grant.

Products and Solutions

Original document handling

On top of SWIM, we are also developing a solution to handle original documents of claims more efficiently and help you save storage space.

Data extraction

Our smart system will use articial intelligence to extract data from documents, saving manpower and reducing the possiblity of human errors.

APIs

We are also developing APIs to integrate with insurer’s systems to avoid repeated data entries, making the claims handling process more efficient.

Data visualization

Our system will also provide data visualizations so you can view sick leave patterns more easily in order to identify fraudulent claims.

A smart system to handle original documents
FEATURES
Storage

Original documents are scanned and kept in a safe place.

Copies

A QR code will be stamped on the “true copy” and passed to relevant parties.

Retrieval

Clients can retrieve copies or the original documents. All handling of documents is logged securely.

Browsing

Useful data can be extracted from the documents and passed to clients through an API or in formats like JSON or CSV.

WORKFLOW
Original documents are scanned and uploaded to our system with a reference and location code. Relevant data is extracted and updated in our system as well.
Clients will receive copies with QR codes pasted on the corner. Clients can scan the QR code to access information on the document.
Clients can request data on the document, ask for another copy, or request the originl document.
